# Ice Traction

## Concept Type

performance_metric

## Definition

Tire traction performance on icy surfaces. In this source, it is introduced mainly as a performance constraint that trades off against [[Snow Traction]] when tread void area is increased.

## Key Claims

- Increasing [[Void Ratio]] decreases ice traction because it reduces the contact area between tire and ice.
- Ice friction is associated with quasi-liquid or melted water layers near the surface, especially near the melting point and under sliding.

## Design Implications

Snow tire design cannot optimize [[Void Ratio]] for snow alone; it must preserve enough contact area and ice-surface friction capability for icy-road performance.
