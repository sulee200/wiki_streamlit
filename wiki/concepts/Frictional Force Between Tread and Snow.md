# Frictional Force Between Tread and Snow

## Concept Type

force

## Definition

The frictional component acting between tire tread rubber and snow surfaces, controlled by tread-rubber friction coefficient and actual contact area.

## Key Claims

- The source approximates this force from the sliding frictional force of a locked smooth tire on compacted snow.
- It contributes to tractive force at both small and large slip ratio in the analytical model.
- The source assumes this force can remain unchanged when estimating added plowing force from sipes.

## Design Implications

Rubber compound and contact area remain important even when tread pattern features dominate snow mechanics. However, increasing void area for snow may reduce available contact area for other surfaces.
