# Plowing Force

## Concept Type

force

## Definition

The force generated when tread block and sipe edges dig into and plow the snow surface as the tire rolls.

## Key Claims

- Plowing force is strongly related to the number of sipes and the total lateral edge length in the footprint.
- The source estimates plowing force per unit edge length at about 10 N/mm for packed snow with top-layer density from 0.3 to 0.5.
- It contributes to traction at both small and large slip ratio.

## Design Implications

Increasing effective lateral edge length can improve snow traction, but the design must avoid compromising block stiffness, contact stability, or ice performance.
